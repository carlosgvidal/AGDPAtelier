Manifesto

The object as document.
Weight as truth.

A GROSS DOMESTIC PRODUCT does not manufacture accessories. It translates social theory, economic abstraction and structural computation into material devices of high precision — physical essays whose final argument is matter.

I — Origin

The name begins with an economic measure.

Gross domestic product reduces collective life to an aggregate: production, circulation, value. The practice takes that impersonal metric and returns it to the body. What is usually expressed as scale becomes weight; what appears as abstraction becomes contact. The object is therefore neither illustration nor ornament. It is a condensed conflict between global systems and singular existence.

II — Method

Thought becomes rule. Rule becomes form.

Each work emerges from a system of constraints: geometry, repetition, interruption, symmetry, variation and structural resistance. Research precedes matter; computation precedes execution. Yet the system does not seek neutral efficiency. It produces objects in which technical precision preserves an unresolved tension — between the reproducible logic of the machine and the irreducible singularity of the result.

III — Object

Jewelry is displaced toward corporal architecture.

The body is not treated as a display surface but as a site of negotiation. Weight, scale and contact make the object present; they prevent it from disappearing into decoration. The piece occupies space, alters posture and reorganizes perception. It does not complete the wearer. It establishes a material relation with them: not adornment, but encounter.

IV — Promise

No object precedes its execution.

A GROSS DOMESTIC PRODUCT delivers technically rigorous objects conceived as anchors of identity and catalysts of reflection. Every piece is produced as a material document: authored, structurally resolved and resistant to the anonymity of mass repetition. The work does not promise timelessness. It promises presence.

Positioning

The practice occupies the intersection between philosophical essay and authored object.

01

Corporal Architecture

The territory is infrastructure, not ornament. Form addresses the body through weight, scale and structural consequence.

02

Conceptual Legitimacy

Value emanates from research and process. Semiotics, economics and computation become material operations rather than external references.

03

Subversion of Luxury

Where conventional luxury promises fantasy, the practice offers density, friction and intellectual sovereignty.

Principles

Five principles govern the practice. None are decorative.

01
Brutalist Rigor

Research precedes matter. Every object condenses an intellectual trajectory into a form stripped of anecdotal ornament.

02
Structural Gravity

Weight is not an inconvenience to be concealed. It is a language: an architectural force through which the object declares its presence.

03
Symbolic Tension

The work exposes the conflict between abstract systems of value and the intimate scale of the body.

04
Honest Materiality

Metal is treated as structure, surface and evidence. Finish clarifies form; it does not disguise it.

05
Authored Singularity

Variation is generated through a system, but meaning remains situated. Every object bears the decisions, limits and responsibility of an author.

Design DNA

A visual grammar of interruption, density and controlled excess.

Anti-Ornament

Decoration is removed until only the operation remains: cut, pressure, repetition, void and structural edge.

Monumental Scale

Even when small, the object behaves as architecture. It occupies rather than embellishes.

Computational Variation

Rules generate families of forms without reducing them to sameness. Finite operations produce singular outcomes.

Material Precision

High polish, industrial texture and exact geometry coexist without nostalgia for traditional ornament.

Authorship / Transparency

The system has an author. The object has a position.

Carlos Adolfo Gutiérrez Vidal
Founder and Creative Director

A GROSS DOMESTIC PRODUCT is an independent practice founded and directed by Carlos Adolfo Gutiérrez Vidal, a Mexican poet, academic, cultural practitioner and conceptual designer whose work moves between semiotics, aesthetics, literature and material culture.

The brand extends that transdisciplinary practice into objects. Its systems, texts and forms are conceived as parts of the same inquiry: how thought acquires weight, how language becomes structure, and how the body negotiates with the forms it chooses to carry.
